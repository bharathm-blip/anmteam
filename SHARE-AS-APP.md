# 📱 Sharing the ANM Portal as an App (No Play Store / App Store needed)

Your portal is a **PWA (Progressive Web App)**. Colleagues can install it directly
from the browser — it gets an icon on their home screen and opens full-screen like
a native app, with no browser address bar.

---

## How colleagues install it

Just share your link (e.g. **https://portal.anmoffice.in** or your vercel.app URL).
Then they do this once:

### On iPhone / iPad (Safari)
1. Open the link in **Safari** (must be Safari, not Chrome)
2. Tap the **Share** button (square with ↑ arrow at the bottom)
3. Scroll down → tap **"Add to Home Screen"**
4. Tap **Add** (top right)
5. ✅ The "ANM Portal" icon now appears on their home screen

### On Android (Chrome)
1. Open the link in **Chrome**
2. Tap the **⋮** menu (top right)
3. Tap **"Add to Home screen"** (or "Install app")
4. Tap **Add / Install**
5. ✅ The "ANM Portal" icon now appears on their home screen

### On Desktop (Chrome / Edge)
1. Open the link
2. Look for an **install icon** (⊕ or a small screen icon) in the address bar
3. Click **Install**
4. ✅ It opens as its own window/app

---

## What they get
- Full-screen app (no browser bar)
- ANM gold app icon
- Bottom navigation bar (like Instagram)
- Their own login — data syncs live
- Works offline for viewing (actions need internet)

---

## Tips for rolling out to your team
1. Send everyone the link + these instructions (or a screenshot of the steps)
2. Give them their email + default password `Anm@2026`
3. They install → log in → set their own password on first login
4. Done — no app store, no approval delays, instant updates when you push changes

---

## Why this is better than Play Store / App Store (for internal tools)
- ✅ **Instant** — no review process (App Store takes days)
- ✅ **Free** — no $99/yr Apple or $25 Google developer fees
- ✅ **Private** — only people with the link can access (and they still need a login)
- ✅ **Auto-updates** — when you push to GitHub, everyone gets the update immediately
- ✅ **One codebase** — works on iPhone, Android, and desktop

You can always publish to the app stores later if you want — but for an internal
office tool, the "Add to Home Screen" approach is ideal.
